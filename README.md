### part B- Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
A practical guide based on real scenarios:

**Do's:**
- Use a professional profile picture (not your party photos!)
- Keep posts respectful and positive — recruiters do check
- Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
- Think before posting: "Would I want my future employer to see this?"
- Share educational content, achievements, and helpful resources

**Don'ts:**
- Never share personal phone number, address, or live location
- Don't engage in online arguments or cyberbullying
- Avoid sharing unverified news or information
- Don't use offensive language in public posts
- Be selective about accepting friend/follow requests

###  Task 5- Cyber Crime Awareness 

### part A- Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud
I researched a real cybercrime that's affecting thousands in India — UPI fraud. This case study covers:

**The Scenario:**
Priya Sharma, a 28-year-old software engineer from Bhopal, lost ₹68,000 in minutes. A fake Swiggy customer care caller convinced her to share OTPs for a "refund."

**How the Scam Works:**
1. Scammers gather basic details through phishing SMS or calls
2. They pose as trusted entities — customer support, delivery agents, bank officials
3. They create urgency and trick victims into sharing OTPs or UPI PINs
4. Funds are instantly transferred through mule accounts

**Impact:**
- Financial loss (₹68,000 in this case — could be more)
- Emotional trauma and stress
- Loss of trust in digital payment systems
- Long, exhausting process with banks and police for recovery

**Prevention:**
- Never share OTPs — no genuine organization asks for them
- Verify calls by calling back on official numbers
- Report immediately to 1930 or cybercrime.gov.in

### part B- Prevention Checklist
A quick-reference checklist for staying safe online:

**General Online Safety:**
- ☐ Never click on links in unsolicited emails or WhatsApp messages
- ☐ Always type official website URLs yourself
- ☐ Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts
- ☐ Never share OTPs, passwords, or UPI PINs with anyone
- ☐ Verify UPI payment requests twice before approving
- ☐ Use only official apps from Play Store / App Store
- ☐ Keep phone and laptop software updated
- ☐ Report suspicious activity to National Cyber Crime Portal or call 1930

**Financial / UPI Safety:**
- ☐ Set a low daily transaction limit in UPI apps
- ☐ Never scan random QR codes for payments
- ☐ Check transaction notifications immediately
- ☐ Link only one bank account to UPI apps if possible

##  Key Takeaways — What I Learned

Working on this project taught me more than I expected:

1. **Digital literacy is a life skill** — not something just for tech professionals. Every student, regardless of branch, needs to understand how to navigate the digital world safely.

2. **Your online presence is your first impression** — before you meet someone, they've probably Googled you. Make sure what they find represents you well.

3. **Cybercrime is closer than we think** — UPI fraud happens to educated professionals too. Awareness is your best defense.

4. **Professional communication matters** — a well-written email can open doors that casual messages never will.

5. **Small habits = big protection** — enabling 2FA, checking links before clicking, setting transaction limits — these simple actions can save you from major trouble.

6. **Teaching helps learning** — creating the quiz and explaining these concepts to others helped me understand them better myself.
